This implies:
\begin{equation}
\sigma_s^2 = \frac{\sigma_\nu^2}{n_s}.
\end{equation}

Assume participation increases with usage:
\begin{equation}
n_s = \bar{n} + \rho q_s, \quad \bar{n} > 0, \ \rho > 0.
\end{equation}

Then:
\begin{equation}
\sigma_s^2(q_s) = \frac{\sigma_\nu^2}{\bar{n} + \rho q_s}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Interpretation}

As stablecoin usage increases, more traders participate and the price aggregates more dispersed information. This reduces noise and increases precision.

\subsection{Implications}

The precision of public information is an equilibrium outcome driven by market participation. This creates a feedback loop between asset demand and information aggregation, which is central to the model's mechanism.
