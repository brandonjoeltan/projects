Quantitative Results (Global-Game Version)

This section evaluates the model using the global-game formulation of the run margin. The objective is to verify that the mechanisms derived in the theory appear in a quantitative environment and to demonstrate how stablecoins affect FX demand, crisis outcomes, and welfare.

Implementation

We solve the model on a grid of overvaluation states theta. For each theta, we evaluate the equilibrium at the on-path public signal s = theta. The model is solved using a fixed-point algorithm over three objects: the run cutoff, the allocation of FX demand across instruments, and public-signal precision.

Foreign-Currency Demand

Total FX demand increases in theta. This is driven by hedge demand at low levels of theta and by run demand at higher levels. Stablecoin demand also increases with theta and represents a growing share of total FX demand.

Crisis Outcomes

The model generates regions with both crisis and non-crisis outcomes. Introducing stablecoins increases crisis incidence by lowering access costs and improving coordination through public information.

Welfare

Welfare is higher with stablecoins at low theta and lower at high theta. This reflects a tradeoff between improved access and increased coordination-driven crisis losses.

Decomposition

Shutting down the cost channel removes welfare gains at low theta. Shutting down the information channel reduces crisis amplification. Both channels are necessary for the full result.
